Yongseok Kwon

I am a robot researcher currently stationed at the Republic of Korea Army Headquarters. Prior to my military service, I had the privilege of working at ROAHM Lab under the guidance of Professor Ram Vasudevan at the University of Michigan. I earned my Bachelor of Science degree in 2020 from UNIST and completed my Master of Science degree in 2022 at the University of Michigan.

My research interests include safe reinforcement learning, active learning, and task and motion planning. My research goal is to develop robust deep learning-based algorithms that can seamlessly integrate autonomous robots into our daily lives, making them reliable companions for a range of tasks.

Projects

Transformer for Motion Planning

Fall 2021 at the University of Michigan
Advised by prof. Nima Fazeli

Deployed the decision transformer to solve the reaching task of a 7 DOF robot arm.

Contact Simulation via Linear Complementary Problem

Fall 2021 at the University of Michigan
Advised by prof. Nima Fazeli

Implemented a contact simulator using the Linear Complementarity Problem (LCP) formulation to model contact dynamics.

Model Predictive Control for Autonomous Car

Fall 2021 at the University of Michigan
Advised by prof. Ram Vasudevan

Developed and implemented a convex collision avoidance constraint for the lane-changing task of an autonomous car.

Extended Kalman Filter on Neural Gait Model

Fall 2021 at the University of Michigan
Advised by prof. Robert D. Gregg

Developed an Extended Kalman Filter on a neural gait model, which was trained using a neural network. Tested the state estimation algorithm on an open-source robotic leg.

UAV Navigation via Dubins Path Planning

Spring 2019 at UNIST
Advised by prof. Hyondong Oh

Developed a Dubins-curve based RRT algorithm to generate dynamically feasible paths for UAVs under kinodynamic constraints. Implemented the underlying dynamics of the simulation and low-level controller.

Dual-Epicyclic Gearset for Decoupling Tendon-Driven Mechanism

2019 Fall at UNIST
Advised by prof. JoonbumBae

Designed a dual-epicyclic gearset to address the issue of motion coupling in multi-DOF tendon-driven mechanisms.
